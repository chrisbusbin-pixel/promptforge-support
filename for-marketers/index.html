The marketer's daily prompt problem

You sit down at 9 AM. You open ChatGPT or Claude. You type out the same 200-word setup prompt you typed yesterday — "you are a senior B2B SaaS copywriter for a target audience of [role] at [industry], your tone is [tone], the goal of this email is..." — and then you give it the actual brief.

An hour later, you do the same for a different campaign. Then again for an ad headline. Then again for a SEO outline. By Friday, you've rewritten the same 6-8 setup prompts dozens of times.

The marketing prompt library you'll build (in roughly priority order)

Cold email templates

You are a senior B2B SaaS sales copywriter writing to a {{role}} at a {{company_size}}-person {{industry}} company. The product is {{product_oneliner}}. The reader's likely pain point is {{pain_point}}. Write a 90-word cold email that: - Opens with a specific observation (not "I noticed you...") - States the value prop in one sentence - Includes one specific proof point - Closes with a single low-friction CTA Tone: direct, no buzzwords, sounds like a human.

Headline brainstorming

Generate 15 headline variations for {{piece_topic}}, targeted at {{audience}}. Mix: - 3 curiosity gap headlines - 3 numbered list headlines - 3 contrarian/provocative headlines - 3 benefit-driven headlines - 3 question-format headlines For each, indicate the angle in one phrase. No clickbait.

Long-form blog outline

Build a detailed outline for a {{word_count}}-word blog post on {{topic}}, targeting {{audience}} with the search intent of {{intent}}. Include: - H1 working title - 4-6 H2 sections with brief summaries - Suggested data/stats to include - 3 internal link opportunities - 5 long-tail keyword variations to address - Suggested CTA Tone: {{tone}}. Reference {{competitor_or_inspiration}} but with stronger {{differentiator}}.

Ad copy variations

Write 8 versions of an ad for {{platform}} targeting {{audience}} for {{product}}. Constraints: - Headline: under {{headline_chars}} characters - Body: under {{body_chars}} characters - Required CTA: {{cta}} Vary across: - Pain-point opener (2 versions) - Aspiration opener (2) - Statistic opener (2) - Question opener (2) For each, indicate which testing hypothesis it represents.

Social post batch

Write 5 {{platform}} posts about {{topic}} for {{audience}}, each: - A different angle (story, contrarian take, list, question, behind-the-scenes) - Native to the platform's voice - Under {{character_limit}} characters - Each ending with a different engagement prompt Avoid: emojis, hashtag stuffing, generic advice.

The 90-day marketing prompt library build

Week 1: Audit + capture

For one week, every time you write a prompt to an AI tool, save it to a notes app. By end of week you'll have 20-40 captured prompts.

Week 2: Convert to templates

Open PromptForge. For each captured prompt, identify the variable parts and replace with {{placeholders}}. Save with a descriptive name. Group into folders by use case.

Weeks 3-4: Refine + add

As you use the templates, you'll notice which ones need polishing. Refine. Add new templates as new use cases appear. By end of month one you'll have 50-80 working templates.

Months 2-3: Settle into rotation

You'll find your top 10 most-used templates. Star them. They're the ones you'll touch daily. The other 70 are situational but still save you the rewriting tax when needed.
