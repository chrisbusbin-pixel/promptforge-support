The forgotten-subscription problem

Apple's built-in subscription manager (Settings → Apple ID → Subscriptions) only tracks things you bought through the App Store. AI tools mostly aren't sold through the App Store — they're billed directly by OpenAI, Anthropic, Midjourney, etc. via credit card.

Result: most AI users have no central view of what they're paying for AI tools. The credit card statement just shows charges from "OPENAI" or "ANTHROPIC" with no context.

The forgotten subscription is the most expensive one. The Midjourney plan you signed up for in March, used for two weeks, never canceled — that's $30/month for 9 months = $270 wasted before you noticed.

The 3 ways to track AI subscriptions

1. A spreadsheet (free, manual)

Columns: Tool name, plan, monthly cost, billing date, last used date. Update monthly. Works fine if you actually maintain it. Most people don't.

2. A general subscription tracker app (Bobby, Subby, Subscriptions)

Tracks all recurring charges, not just AI. Often subscription-based itself (irony). Good if you want to see total household subscription spend.

The 4-step audit (do this once a quarter)

Step 1: List every AI tool you've signed up for

Check email for "Welcome to..." messages from AI tools over the past 12 months. Check credit card statement for AI-related charges (search for company names). The list is usually longer than you remember.

Step 2: For each tool, answer one question

"Did I use this in the past 30 days?" Yes = keep. No = strong cancel candidate. Maybe = downgrade to free tier and see if you actually miss it.

Step 3: Cancel the dead ones immediately

Don't wait. Don't think "I might use it next month." If you didn't use it last month, you won't use it next month. Cancel.

Step 4: Re-audit in 90 days

Most people add 1–2 new AI subscriptions per quarter. Without quarterly audits, the stack creeps up to $200/month.

The "is it worth it" tests

Per tool, ask:

  • Frequency: Use 3+ times/week? Worth it. Once a week or less? Free tier.
  • Replaceability: Could I get the same value from a free tool with slightly worse UX? Often yes — most paid AI tools have free alternatives.
  • Stack overlap: Am I paying ChatGPT Plus AND Claude Pro? One usually suffices for most people.
  • Annual math: $20/month is $240/year. Would I write a $240 check today for this? Often a no.
