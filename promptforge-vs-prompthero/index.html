PromptForge vs PromptHero

PromptHero is a marketplace where people sell and discover prompts. PromptForge is a private prompt library that lives on your phone. They solve different problems.

The fundamental difference

PromptHero is a marketplace. Other people upload prompts. You browse, buy, sell, vote, comment. It's social. It's a discovery engine. If your goal is to find prompts other people have created — especially for image generation in Midjourney/Stable Diffusion — PromptHero has a huge library.

PromptForge is a private library. You save your own prompts (or import them from PromptHero or anywhere else). They live on your device. You organize them in folders. You use variable templates. You run them whenever you want without an internet connection.

Use PromptHero to discover. Use PromptForge to store and reuse. Many people use both.

When to choose PromptForge

  • You want a private library, not a public marketplace
  • You want offline access on your phone
  • You don't want a subscription
  • You don't want to create another account
  • You want variable-based templates with `{{placeholder}}` fill-in
  • You want your prompts on your device, not a server
  • You also want to track what you're spending on AI subscriptions

When to choose PromptHero

  • You want to discover what others are creating
  • You want to sell your own prompts
  • You're focused on Midjourney/Stable Diffusion image generation
  • Community ratings and votes matter to you

Use both

Browse PromptHero for inspiration. When you find a prompt you want to keep using, save it to PromptForge. PromptForge then becomes your personal vault — organized, taggable with custom emoji-iconned folders, and yours forever for $4.99.
