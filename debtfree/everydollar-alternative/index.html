What each app is actually built for

EveryDollar is a full zero-based budgeting app. Track every dollar of income, assign it to a category, watch the budget zero out at the end of the month. Built around Dave Ramsey's "every dollar has a job" philosophy. Premium tier auto-imports bank transactions.

DebtFree is a focused debt payoff planner. Add your debts (balances, rates, minimums) manually. Pick snowball or avalanche. See your debt-free date in real time. Use the what-if tool to test extra payments.

DebtFree is not a substitute for a budgeting app. If you need full income/spending tracking, you need EveryDollar, YNAB, Monarch, or similar. DebtFree is for the specific job of getting out of debt with a clear plan.

Why some people prefer no bank login

Bank linking via Plaid (which EveryDollar Premium uses) is convenient, but:

  • Your transaction data flows through a third party
  • You're trusting both EveryDollar AND Plaid with your financial credentials
  • If either service has a breach, your account info is exposed
  • Some banks don't support Plaid, leaving you with manual entry anyway

DebtFree's design says: you don't need bank linking to plan a debt payoff. You enter balances manually. The plan still works. The math is the same. You just keep your bank credentials private.
